Ban Zhang Qiao Mu 2016 is a well-balanced semi-aged sheng puerh from the renowned Ban Zhang mountain region. Known as the "King of Puerh," Ban Zhang tea is famous for its intense energy and complex flavor transformation. This 2016 vintage offers a perfect introduction to the region's character, softened by time.

## Origin

Ban Zhang mountain region, Yunnan, China. This tea comes from Qiao Mu (Arbor) trees, which typically denote older, ecological tea garden trees. The Ban Zhang terroir is famous for producing teas with a strong mouthfeel, pronounced bitterness that turns into sweetness (hui gan), and a commanding "Cha Qi."

## Flavor & Aroma Profile

- **Aroma**: Slightly smoky with underlying floral and fruity notes.
- **Taste**: Bright yet soft and easy to drink. It features a characteristic slight smokiness interlaced with floral notes. A pleasant bitterness exists in the background, quickly transforming into sweetness.
- **Finish**: Refreshing and lingering.

## Effects & Benefits

**Tea Superpowers:**
- Toning and refreshing
- Strong "Cha Qi" typical of Ban Zhang teas
- Cleanses the palate and invigorates the senses
- Semi-aged character offers a balance between the vibrance of youth and the mellowness of age

## Brewing Instructions

**Simple brewing method:**
- **For 300-500ml teapot**: 5-7g of tea
- **Water temperature**: Boiling water (98-100°C)
- **Steeping time**: 3-5 minutes

It is strong, so don't let it steep too long if you prefer a milder taste.
